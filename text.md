### Company Background:

The Fraunhofer-Gesellschaft was founded in Munich on March 26, 1949, as part of a
program to reorganize and expand Germany's research infrastructure. The organization takes
its name from Joseph von Fraunhofer (1787 - 1826), the successful Munich researcher,
inventor, and entrepreneur. In its early years, the main function of this non-profit organization
was predominantly administrative: to raise funds through government bodies, donations, and
association members for distribution to research projects of relevance to industry.
Initial activities primarily focused on industry in Bavaria. This being the early post-war period,
there was a particular need for research in the fields of mining, the iron and steel industry, and
mechanical engineering. Over the decades, Fraunhofer has grown into Europe's largest
application-oriented research organization, with 72 institutes and research units spread across
Germany. The organization's research efforts now cover a broad spectrum of scientific


disciplines and applications, aiming to drive innovation and foster technological advancements
that benefit industry and society.
Fraunhofer FOKUS, where I interned, is one of the institutes under the Fraunhofer umbrella. It
focuses on research and development in the areas of smart cities, digital networking, and
communication systems. The institute collaborates with partners from industry, academia, and
the public sector to develop solutions that address current and future challenges in these
domains. My role at Fraunhofer FOKUS was centered around the development of mobile and
web applications, particularly in the context of media streaming and the Metaverse, providing
a unique opportunity to contribute to cutting-edge research and innovation.
